03

The physical card

The thing that sits on the counter. It needs to be eye-catching and graphic — packaging rules, not UI rules.

Current
Proposed
The card stays the same.

Packaging needs to be eye-catching. The illustrative card is the right call for the physical product — it just lives in a more refined surrounding system.

What changes

  • Type — drop Archivo Black + ALL CAPS for body. Use Fraunces (editorial display) + Inter Tight (UI sans).
  • Borders & shadows — replace 3px black outlines and hard offset shadows with hairline borders and soft drop shadows.
  • Color — keep the warm palette, use it sparingly. Cream and white as primary surfaces, tomato/honey as accents.
  • Mascot placement — Shmo lives in marketing moments (heroes, mailers, packaging) — not in the dashboard chrome.
  • Microcopy — sentence case, real numbers, real timestamps. Less "TAP. REVIEW. GET FOUND." more "Turn happy customers into 5-star reviews."

What stays

  • Shmo the mascot — same character, same warmth. Just used more selectively.
  • The warm palette — cream, honey, tomato, navy. Same colors, more whitespace.
  • The physical card — flat illustrative graphic stays as-is. It's packaging, and packaging benefits from being graphic and bold.
  • The personality — friendly, confident, on-your-side. Not corporate, not stuffy. Just trustworthy.
  • References — Linear, Stripe, Mailchimp, Cal.com. Brands that are warm AND credible.
