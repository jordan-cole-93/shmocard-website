# ShmoCard — Voice, Copy & Proof

This document is the writing toolkit. It contains the voice rules, the locked headlines that cannot be changed, available copy fragments you can pull from, the verified proof points, and the FAQ. Treat it like a content library — not a script.

---

## Voice rules

Plainspoken. Warm. Confident. Like a co-worker who's good at the thing — not a marketer.

- **Short sentences.** Most under 12 words.
- **Real nouns.** "The card behind the counter" beats "the single-unit deployment."
- **No hedging.** Cut "may," "could potentially," "depending on."
- **Second person.** "You" beats "customers" or "users."
- **Concrete numbers.** "~15 reviews/week" beats "more reviews."
- **No fluff transitions.** Cut "in today's world," "let's face it," "the truth is."

### We never sound like

| Style | Banned phrases |
|---|---|
| Tech-bro | ship, vibe, 10x, stack |
| Generic SaaS | seamless, frictionless, robust, enterprise-grade, leverage |
| Funnel marketer | crushing it, you're leaving money on the table |
| Apologetic | "we know reviews can be tricky, but…" |

### The tradesperson test

Before publishing any line of copy:
> **Would a barber, a roofer, or a pawnshop owner feel respected reading this?**

If it talks down, assumes they don't know their business, or sounds like a marketing deck — rewrite it.

## The bulk math argument (the spine of every page)

The single argument the entire site is built to make:

| Setup | Reviews per week |
|---|---|
| One card behind the counter | ~2 |
| One card per employee | ~15 |

A ~7× increase. Bulk solves the problem. Single units do not. Show the math — do not soften it.

Phrasings of this argument that work:
- "One per employee, not one per shop."
- "The card has to live with the person handing the customer their coffee, keys, or receipt — not on the counter."
- "Behind the counter: ~2 a week. In every employee's pocket: ~15."
- "Built for crews. Priced for bulk."

## Messaging angles

Different visitors arrive with different pain. Pull from any of these depending on the moment.

### Nurturing angles (warm the problem)

| Angle | Who it speaks to | The pain |
|---|---|---|
| **Employee Friction** | Owners whose employees won't ask for reviews | Asking out loud is awkward — employees avoid it. A card removes the ask. |
| **Competitor Gap** | Owners losing to a competitor with more reviews | A nearby shop ranks higher and gets more walk-ins. Reviews are why. |
| **Search Invisibility** | Owners who don't show up locally on Google | They're invisible on Maps and don't understand why. Review count is a major factor. |
| **Customer Effort** | Owners asking but not getting reviews | Customers intend to leave one but give up — too many steps from "I'll do it later" to actually posting. |
| **Trust Deficit** | Owners not getting enough new customers | Not enough reviews = not enough trust = fewer first-time customers. |

Angles 1 (Employee Friction) and 4 (Customer Effort) are strongest for bulk orders.

### Direct-offer angles (close)

- **Demo Walkthrough** — show the tap live, the product sells itself.
- **Stats Drop** — lead with a verified number.

### How it works (compressed)

1. You order cards — one per employee.
2. We program each card with your Google review link before shipping.
3. Your employee hands the card to the customer after the transaction.
4. Customer taps the card with their phone.
5. Their Google review page opens instantly. They tap five stars and type.
6. A QR code on the back covers any phone that can't tap.

## FAQ (use verbatim or lightly edited)

| ID | Question | Answer |
|---|---|---|
| `what` | What is Shmo Review? | An NFC card, sign or disc that, when tapped by a customer's phone, opens your Google review page directly. No app, no login, no QR-code gymnastics. |
| `phone` | Does it work with every phone? | iPhone XS and newer (2018+) and Android 5.0 and newer have NFC built in — customers tap and the review page opens. A QR code on the back covers any phone that can't. |
| `reprog` | Can I change where the card sends people? | Yes — every card is reprogrammable for life. Update the destination URL from the dashboard whenever you want. |
| `bulk` | Why one card per crew member? | A card behind the counter captures roughly 2 reviews a week. One per employee captures roughly 15. The card has to live with the person handing the customer their coffee, keys, or receipt — not on the counter. |
| `ship` | How fast does it ship? | Orders placed by Tuesday 5pm CT ship Friday. Standard shipping is 3 days; bulk orders (10+) ship free expedited. |
| `google-link` | How do I get my Google review link? | Search your business in Google Maps, click "Ask for reviews," and copy the short link. Send it to us when you order, and every card ships pre-programmed. |
| `returns` | What if my crew hates it? | 30-day no-questions returns. Box it back up and we'll refund the full amount. |

Additional FAQ candidates (write fresh if useful):
- "What's on the card — is it our logo?" *(yes, every order is custom-printed with the shop's brand)*
- "Do customers need an app?" *(no — the review page just opens in their phone's browser)*
- "What about Apple devices that don't unlock to NFC?" *(QR fallback handles every edge case)*

## A few "do nots"

- Do not promise a specific review count to a specific buyer ("you'll get 50 reviews next week"). The verified results are aggregate.
- Do not write copy that limits the audience to the US, "small towns," or a single industry.
- Do not reference internal systems (Shopify, Storefront API, GHL) in customer-facing copy.
- Do not call the cards "smart cards," "tech-enabled cards," or any phrase that introduces complexity. They are NFC cards. People tap them.
- Do not make the homepage a Shmo Review shop page. It promotes all four products with equal weight, even the three that are coming soon.
