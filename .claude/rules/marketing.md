## Voice rules

Plainspoken. Warm. Confident. Sound like a co-worker who's good at the thing — not a marketer.

- **Short sentences.** Most under 12 words.
- **Real nouns.** "The card behind the counter" beats "the single-unit deployment."
- **No hedging.** Cut "may", "could potentially", "depending on".
- **Second person.** "You" beats "customers" or "users".
- **Concrete numbers.** "~15 reviews/week" beats "more reviews."

**We don't sound like:**
- Tech bro: "ship", "vibe", "10x", "stack"
- Generic SaaS: "seamless", "frictionless", "robust", "enterprise-grade", "leverage"
- Funnel-bro: "crushing it", "you're leaving money on the table"
- Apologetic: "we know reviews can be tricky, but..."

## The bulk math argument

The single argument the entire site is built to make:

| Setup | Reviews/week |
|---|---|
| One card behind the counter | ~2 |
| One card per employee | ~15 |

~7× increase. Bulk solves the problem. Single units don't. Show the math — don't soften it.

## Tradesperson test

Before publishing any copy:
> **Would a barber, a roofer, or a pawnshop owner feel respected reading this?**

If it talks down, assumes they don't know their business, or sounds like a marketing deck — rewrite it.

## Messaging angles

**Nurturing (warm the problem):**

| Angle | Who | Pain |
|---|---|---|
| Employee Friction | Owners whose employees won't ask for reviews | Asking out loud is awkward — employees avoid it |
| Competitor Gap | Owners losing to a competitor with more reviews | They rank higher, get more walk-ins |
| Search Invisibility | Owners who don't show up locally | Invisible on Google, don't understand why |
| Customer Effort | Owners asking but not getting reviews | Customers intend to but give up — too many steps |
| Trust Deficit | Owners not getting enough new customers | Not enough reviews = not enough trust |

**Direct offer (close):**
- Demo Walkthrough — show the tap live, product sells itself
- Stats Drop — lead with a verified number

Angles 1 (Employee Friction) and 4 (Customer Effort) are strongest for bulk orders.
