# Wireframe — Homepage (`/`)

**Marketing angle:** Shmocard is the crew toolkit for local businesses. Not one product — a family of tools, each handling a different customer interaction that used to get lost. The homepage introduces the brand and routes visitors to the right product. It does not lead with reviews only.

**Tone:** Warm, plainspoken, confident. Tradesperson test on every line — would a barber or pawnshop owner feel respected?

---

**Intent:** Brand-level introduction. Establishes Shmocard as a toolkit ecosystem, not a single product. Does not anchor to NFC or reviews.

**Headline (locked):** "The toolkit your crew's been missing."

**Subhead (draft):** "Four tools built for Main Street. Reviews, contacts, links, reputation — every customer interaction your crew was letting slip through the cracks."

## Section 3 — Why Shmocard

**Intent:** The unifying pitch. What makes Shmocard different from buying 4 separate tools from 4 different companies. Warm, benefit-led, passes the tradesperson test.

**Headline (draft):** "Simple tools. Real results. No subscription on the cards."

**3 pillars (icons + short copy):**

1. **Built for the whole crew** — Not just the owner. One tool per employee means 7× more interactions captured. Every team member has what they need.

2. **No apps. No logins. No friction.** — Customers tap, scan, or click. That's it. No account creation, no download, no fumbling.

3. **One family. Not four subscriptions.** — Every Shmocard tool works together. Early adopters get grandfathered into new tools as they launch.

---

## Section 4 — Shmo Review Spotlight

**Intent:** Deep dive on the flagship product. This is where the bulk math argument lives. Visitors who came for reviews find their full answer here.

**Headline (locked):** "One tap. One five-star review."

**Sub-angle:** The bulk math argument — one card per shop vs. one per employee.

**Sections within the spotlight:**
1. How it works — 3 steps: Hand the card → Customer taps → Review page opens. Done.
2. The bulk math — visual comparison: 1 card behind the counter = ~2 reviews/week. 1 per employee = ~15 reviews/week. Show the math.
3. Three formats — CR-80 wallet card / L-Sign counter standee / Square card. Each with photo and brief description.
4. Real results strip — +86% Garden City · +81% Buffalo Jewelry · 5 reviews in 2 hours (Marshall)

**CTA:** "Shop Shmo Review →" → `/shmo-review`

## Section 5 — Shmo Biz Spotlight

**Intent:** Introduce Shmo Biz as the business identity tool. Lighter than Shmo Review — coming soon, so no purchase CTA. Generate interest and capture waitlist.
